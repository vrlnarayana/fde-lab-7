## Scope
Adds an automated electronic AKI alert driven by laboratory creatinine trends.

## Detection and Staging
KDIGO staging retained and now also uses urine output criteria: less than 0.5 mL/kg/hour for 6 hours signals Stage 1. The electronic alert fires automatically on a qualifying creatinine rise.

## Common Causes
Pre-renal causes still dominate. Add immune checkpoint inhibitor nephritis to the differential for oncology patients.

## Management
Fluid strategy now emphasises balanced crystalloid and careful assessment to avoid overload, especially in heart failure (see Heart Failure Pathway). Stop nephrotoxins and review contrast.

## Drug Dosing
Daily eGFR review during AKI. Vancomycin and gentamicin require level monitoring; see the Antimicrobial Stewardship Pathway dose bands.

## Referral
Stage 3 AKI, refractory hyperkalaemia, severe acidosis, or fluid overload unresponsive to treatment require urgent nephrology and critical care review.
